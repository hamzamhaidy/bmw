A Note on Roasting

Roasting coffee transforms the chemical and physical properties of raw coffee beans into roasted coffee products. This process is what produces the characteristic flavor of coffee by causing the green coffee beans to change in taste. Many coffee roasters determine the level of roast by eye, assessing the color of the beans to determine the desired roast-level.


History and Origins

The Origins of BMW M Series

The BMW M Series, created by BMW's Motorsport division, debuted in the 1970s with the legendary BMW M1. Originally developed for racing, it became a line of high-performance road cars known for their precision engineering and dynamic driving capabilities.

Performance and Engineering

Engineering Excellence of M Series

M Series cars are equipped with advanced engines, such as twin-turbo inline-6s and V8s, and feature enhancements like lightweight materials, sport-tuned suspensions, and improved aerodynamics. They are built for speed, agility, and luxury.

Iconic Models

BMW M Series Iconic Models

Some famous models in the M Series include the M3, a high-performance sedan and coupe; the M5, a luxury sport sedan; and the X5 M, a powerful SUV. Each model balances power, style, and everyday usability.

M Series Features

Distinctive Features of M Series

Unique design elements, such as aggressive front grilles, quad exhausts, M badging, and signature blue-and-red striped accents, distinguish M Series vehicles. They also feature performance-focused interiors with sport seats, advanced tech, and premium materials.

The BMW M2 (F87)

launched in 2015 as the successor to the BMW 1 Series M Coupé, continues the legacy of powerful compact coupés inspired by the BMW 2002 Turbo and M3 (E30). It features a turbocharged 3.0-litre 6-cylinder engine with 370 hp, later upgraded to 410 hp in the M2 Competition (2018–2020) and 450 hp in the limited M2 CS (2020). Its distinctive design includes flared wings and large air intakes, while M suspension ensures precise handling and driving pleasure. Production ran from 2015 to 2020.

BMW M3 (E30/2S, E30/2SC, E36/2S, E36/2CS, E36/4S, E46/2S, E46/2CS, E90/92/93, F80, G80).

The BMW M3, produced since 1986, began as a road version of a racing car and has evolved across six generations. Early models, like the E30, featured a 2.3-litre 4-cylinder engine with up to 238 hp. The second generation (E36) introduced a 6-cylinder engine with up to 321 hp, while the third (E46) maintained a lightweight, dynamic design with 343 hp. The fourth generation (E90/E92/E93) introduced a 420 hp V8 engine, reaching 280 km/h with the M Driver's Package. The fifth generation (F80) switched to a turbocharged 6-cylinder engine with 431 hp, rising to 450 hp in the M3 Competition. The sixth generation (G80), launched in 2021, features a bold design, offering up to 510 hp in the M3 Competition with an 8-speed M Steptronic transmission. Production: 1986–present Engines: 2.3–4.0 litres, 4, 6, and 8 cylinders, 200–510 hp Dimensions: Length 4346–4794 mm, Width 1680–1887 mm, Height 1370–1431 mm.

BMW M4 (F82, F83, G82).

The BMW M4, introduced in 2014 as the successor to the 3 Series Coupé, features a distinct design with a longer bonnet, shorter overhangs, and a recessed occupant cell for a more powerful look. Early models include the M4 Coupé with 431 hp, the Competition version with 450 hp, the CS with 460 hp, and the GTS Coupé with 500 hp. The second generation features bold vertical kidney grilles, with a 3.0-litre M TwinPower Turbo engine delivering 480 hp in the manual version and 510 hp in the M4 Competition. With the optional M Driver’s Package, it reaches 290 km/h, combining precision, agility, and dynamic performance. Production: 2014–present Engines: 3.0-litre 6-cylinder, 431–510 hp Dimensions: Length 4671–4794 mm, Width 1870–1887 mm, Height 1383–1394 mm.
